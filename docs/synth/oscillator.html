Oscillators

An oscillator is a simple repeating signal. In synthesizers they're the starting point for the soundâ€”an oscillator on its own sounds terrible, but after some filtering, combining, and other transformations you might have something nice. Another use is as a sort of control value: one oscillator might set the frequency of another (like a vibrato) or its volume (tremolo).

Harmonics

Why does nothing use only the even harmonics? Well, the odd harmonics are 1, 3, 5, and so on, with the 1 giving the frequency of the oscillator. The even ones 2, 4, 6 are all multiples of 2â€”this is really an oscillator with all harmonics but at twice the frequency!
